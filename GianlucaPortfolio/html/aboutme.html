Here's who I am & what I do

I am a young aspiring full stack developer. It is not easy to tell in a few words and to be fully understood but it is a challenge that I accept and hope to overcome. I was born and raised in a very retrograde suburban village which allowed me to see what I didn't want to become and where I didn't want to live. While I was growing up I began to have a natural propensity for computer science already in middle school with small programs in Scratch. In high school I started to approach the world of programming knowing guys with the same passion I started studying my first programming language C. Given the pace at which information was given to us, I decided to study alone to carry on and by the end of the second year of high school I acquired my first programming language. During the third year I was very attracted to the idea of ​​programming a site where the students of my school could sell used books instead of wandering between classes as if we were in the postwar period. Thanks to this purpose, I approached web programming and found pure love in it. In the same year in February of my 15th year I enrolled in Cyberchallenge 2020 edition which saw me among the finalists for the national competition of this university project by CINI. Here I approached people and teachers who made me grow from many points of view and lesson after lesson, race after race, I matured by acquiring news and awareness of how big information technology is and us little rowing boats in an ocean of knowledge. I have always dedicated myself to doing a lot of jobs during the summers to be able to afford the economic tranquility that allowed me to continue my independent study path, as a waiter in bars and restaurants, flyers, freelancing, tutoring ...

At the end of this period of about a year I had acquired knowledge of web programming, cybersecurity and scripting in python. Now that I'm here in front of the computer I am 17 years old and still many things to learn,in my life I am sure of one thing you could also turn off the sun but as long as I have computer science I will never be in the dark
